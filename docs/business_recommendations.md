# Business Recommendations

## Executive Overview

The statistical analysis identified several operational and customer service factors that significantly influence customer churn. Rather than focusing on demographic characteristics, the findings indicate that customer retention can be improved by addressing contract structures, service adoption, payment behaviors, and early customer engagement.

The recommendations below prioritize initiatives based on both their statistical impact and potential business value.

---

# Recommendation 1: Reduce Month-to-Month Contract Churn

## Finding

Contract type exhibited the strongest association with customer churn (Cramer's V = 0.410). Customers on month-to-month contracts were substantially more likely to leave than those with one- or two-year agreements.

## Recommendation

Develop targeted retention campaigns for month-to-month customers before their renewal period.

Potential initiatives include:

- Loyalty discounts
- Limited-time contract upgrade offers
- Personalized retention incentives
- Renewal reminder campaigns

## Expected Business Impact

Increasing the proportion of long-term contracts can improve customer retention, stabilize recurring revenue, and reduce customer acquisition costs.

---

# Recommendation 2: Increase Adoption of Support Services

## Finding

Support service adoption showed one of the strongest relationships with customer churn (Cramer's V = 0.369). Customers without technical support or online security services churned at considerably higher rates.

## Recommendation

Promote value-added services during customer onboarding and throughout the customer lifecycle.

Possible strategies include:

- Free trial periods
- Bundled service packages
- Personalized upgrade recommendations
- Customer education campaigns

## Expected Business Impact

Higher adoption of support services can strengthen customer engagement while increasing recurring revenue through additional service subscriptions.

---

# Recommendation 3: Improve Fiber Optic Customer Experience

## Finding

Fiber optic customers experienced higher churn despite subscribing to premium internet services.

This pattern may indicate unmet customer expectations regarding service quality, pricing, or support.

## Recommendation

Conduct additional customer research to identify the underlying causes of dissatisfaction.

Potential initiatives include:

- Customer satisfaction surveys
- Service quality monitoring
- Pricing reviews
- Technical support improvements

## Expected Business Impact

Improving customer satisfaction among premium subscribers can reduce churn within one of the company's highest-value customer segments.

---

# Recommendation 4: Encourage Automatic Payment Methods

## Finding

Customers using electronic check payments churned significantly more frequently than customers using automatic payment methods.

## Recommendation

Encourage customers to adopt automatic payment options through:

- Billing incentives
- Simplified enrollment
- Promotional discounts
- Digital account management improvements

## Expected Business Impact

Automatic payments can improve customer convenience, reduce payment-related issues, and strengthen long-term customer relationships.

---

# Recommendation 5: Strengthen Early Customer Retention

## Finding

Customer tenure demonstrated the strongest numerical relationship with churn (Rank-Biserial Correlation = 0.480). Customers who left the company typically had much shorter tenures than retained customers.

## Recommendation

Focus retention efforts during the first year of the customer lifecycle.

Possible initiatives include:

- Structured onboarding programs
- Welcome communications
- Early satisfaction surveys
- Proactive customer support
- Retention outreach for at-risk customers

## Expected Business Impact

Reducing early customer churn increases customer lifetime value while lowering the costs associated with replacing lost customers.

---

# Strategic Priorities

Based on the statistical analysis, recommended initiatives should be prioritized as follows:

| Priority | Initiative | Business Impact |
|----------|------------|-----------------|
| High | Reduce month-to-month contract churn | Very High |
| High | Increase support service adoption | High |
| High | Improve early customer retention | High |
| Medium | Improve fiber optic customer experience | Medium |
| Medium | Increase automatic payment adoption | Medium |

---

# Business Value

Implementing these recommendations can provide several measurable business benefits:

- Lower customer churn
- Increased customer lifetime value (CLV)
- Higher recurring revenue
- Improved customer satisfaction
- Greater adoption of premium services
- Reduced customer acquisition costs

---

# Future Opportunities

This analysis focused on identifying statistically significant drivers of customer churn using historical customer data.

Future work could extend this project by:

- Developing predictive machine learning models for churn prediction
- Deploying real-time churn risk dashboards using Power BI
- Performing customer segmentation for targeted marketing campaigns
- Evaluating the effectiveness of retention strategies through A/B testing
- Incorporating additional customer behavior and usage data into predictive models

These enhancements would enable the company to transition from descriptive analytics to predictive and prescriptive decision-making, supporting more proactive customer retention strategies.
