# Executive Summary

Customer churn is one of the most significant challenges facing subscription-based telecommunications companies. Losing existing customers not only reduces recurring revenue but also increases customer acquisition costs and impacts long-term profitability.

This project simulates a business analytics engagement focused on identifying customer segments with elevated churn risk, understanding the factors associated with customer attrition, and developing evidence-based retention strategies. Using Excel, Python, Snowflake SQL, and Power BI, the project follows an end-to-end analytics workflow including data quality assessment, exploratory analysis, statistical validation, KPI development, and executive dashboard reporting.

The objective is to transform raw customer data into actionable business insights that support strategic decision-making and improve customer retention efforts.

---

# Business Scenario

A telecommunications company has experienced an increase in customer churn over the past several months. While historical customer data is available, management lacks clear visibility into which customer groups are most likely to leave and which business factors are associated with churn.

The executive leadership team has engaged a data analyst to investigate customer behavior, identify meaningful trends, quantify revenue risk, and provide recommendations that can guide customer retention initiatives.

---

# Business Problem

Customer attrition directly impacts recurring revenue, increases marketing and acquisition expenses, and reduces customer lifetime value.

Without a clear understanding of churn patterns and customer behavior, management cannot effectively prioritize retention campaigns or allocate resources toward the highest-risk customer segments.

The organization requires a data-driven understanding of customer churn to support strategic planning and operational decision-making.

# Business Objectives

The primary objectives of this analysis are to:

- Assess overall customer churn performance.
- Identify customer characteristics associated with higher churn rates.
- Evaluate service, contract, payment, and demographic patterns.
- Quantify potential revenue risk associated with customer attrition.
- Develop key performance indicators (KPIs) for executive monitoring.
- Provide actionable recommendations to improve customer retention.

# Business Constraints

This analysis is subject to several limitations:

- Historical data only; no future customer behavior is observed.
- The dataset represents a single snapshot in time.
- Customer behavior may be influenced by external factors not included in the dataset.
- Observed relationships should be interpreted as associations rather than causal effects.

---

# Assumptions

This project assumes that:

- Customer records accurately represent historical business operations.
- Recorded churn outcomes are correct.
- Missing values have been appropriately addressed during data preparation.
- Available variables sufficiently represent customer characteristics for exploratory analysis.

# Analytical Questions

This project seeks to answer the following business questions:

1. What is the overall customer churn rate?
2. Which customer segments exhibit the highest churn?
3. How does contract type relate to customer retention?
4. Does customer tenure influence churn behavior?
5. Which payment methods are associated with higher churn?
6. How do internet service offerings relate to churn?
7. Which services appear most associated with customer retention?
8. Which customer groups contribute the greatest revenue risk?
9. Which KPIs should executives monitor to improve retention?
